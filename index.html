By: Samuel Rodriguez

Introduction:¶

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease that rapidly spread across the world. It is a respiratory illness that is spread from person to person though close contact. COVID has completely changed the world forever and has affected every single individual differently. Currently COVID has infected over 163.61 million people worldwide with 3.39 million people dying from the virus. Now that we are hopefully nearing the end of the pandemic, it is important to look at who handled the virus the best in order to see how to deal with future pandemics that are bound to happen. In fact, epidemics and even pandemics are becoming increasingly more common with examples such as, Ebola, Zika, SARS, H1N1 flu, and now COVID.

Dataset: I used the website linked in the rubric to find my datasets (https://datascience.nih.gov/covid-19-open-access-resources). I chose to use the Amazon Web Services (AWS) data. This is "a centralized repository of up-to-date and curated datasets related to the spread and characteristics of and COVID-19." Then, I decided to use one dataset that gathered information on the United States at a state level and my other dataset which gathered information on the world at a country level. This gets me all the information I will need.

Mortality Rates¶

The mortality rate is the measure of the number of deaths in a population, scaled to the size of that population, per unit of time. This will give us an accurate representation of how COVID has affected each state/country. In order to find the mortality rate, you divide the total amount of deaths caused by the virus by the population and then multiply this number by 1000.

Conclusion¶

Overall, states/countries that took immediate, swift action to try and prevent the spread of COVID had a much lower infection rate than states/countries that chose to ignore it. This includes national lockdowns, mask mandates, travel restrictions, border closers, contact tracing, and finally stay-at-home orders. These laws and restrictions implemented early allows countries to get back to normal life way sooner than countries who ignored the virus and are still dealing with it today. For example, countries such as New Zealand have already returned back to normal life months ago as compared to the USA who is just starting to return back to normal life now. This shows that it is essential to rapidly respond to a future pandemic that is bound to happen. Not only is it important to respond early, but the state/country must not let up restrictions until the pandemic is fully over. As seen in California and India who had a good start to containing the virus, but let up too early and had masssive outbreaks. Finally, it is important to get vaccinated as soon as possible to further stop the spread of the virus.
